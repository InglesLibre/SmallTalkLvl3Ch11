Making Small Talk

Breaking the Ice & Ending a Conversation

Lesson objective: Use present simple, past simple, and present perfect to discuss hobbies and interests in social situations.

Warm-Up: Discussion

Think about these questions. Share your answers with a partner.

  • Have you ever been to a social event where you didn't know anyone?
  • What did you say to start a conversation?
  • What do you usually talk about with new people?

Key Vocabulary: Social Expressions

  • Break the ice – start a conversation with someone new
  • Small talk – polite conversation about unimportant topics
  • Get into – become interested in or start a hobby

Context: Small Talk at a Naval Event

Read the conversation between two naval trainees who meet at a social event.

A: Hi there. Is this seat taken?

B: No, please sit down. I'm ____________________, by the way.

A: Nice to meet you. I'm ____________________. Are you enjoying the event?

B: Yes, it's great. But I don't know many people here.

A: Me neither. So, what do you do in your free time?

B: Well, I like swimming and I play chess when I have time. What about you?

A: I enjoy going to the theater and I also play golf on weekends.

B: Really? When did you get into that?

A: I got into it about three years ago. My uncle introduced me to it. What about you? When did you start swimming?

B: I've been swimming since I was a child. I used to compete in school.

A: That's impressive! Do you still compete?

B: No, not anymore. Now I just swim for fun. I also belong to a book club.

A: Oh, interesting. What kind of books do you read?

B: Mostly historical fiction and adventure stories. They're my favorites.

A: I like to travel, so I enjoy reading books about different countries.

B: That sounds nice. Have you traveled much?

A: A few times. I visited Brazil and Colombia last year.

B: Amazing! I'd love to travel more.

A: Well, it was great talking to you. I should go and get another drink. Let's continue this later.

B: Sure, I'd like that. It was nice meeting you.

A: You too. See you around.

B: See you later.

Comprehension Questions

Answer the following questions based on the dialogue.

  1. What does B like to do in their free time?
  2. What does A enjoy doing?
  3. When did A get into playing golf?
  4. How long has B been swimming?
  5. What kind of books does B like to read?
  6. What countries did A visit last year?
  7. How does A end the conversation?
  8. Have you ever made small talk at a social event? What did you talk about?

Useful Expressions

  • What do you do in your free time? = What are your hobbies?
  • When did you get into that? = When did you start that hobby?
  • How long have you been doing that? = How long have you had that hobby?

Grammar Focus: Present Simple for Hobbies

We use the present simple to talk about regular activities and hobbies.

Examples from the dialogue:

  • "I like swimming."
  • "I play chess when I have time."
  • "I belong to a book club."
  • "I enjoy going to the theater."

Form: Subject + verb (base form / -s for he/she/it)

  • I play golf.
  • She reads historical fiction.
  • They belong to a book club.

Grammar Focus: Past Simple & Present Perfect

We use the past simple for completed actions and present perfect for experiences and duration.

Examples from the dialogue:

  • Past Simple: "I got into it about three years ago."
  • Past Simple: "I visited Brazil and Colombia last year."
  • Present Perfect: "I have been swimming since I was a child."
  • Present Perfect: "Have you traveled much?"
Tense Use Example
Past Simple Completed action at a specific time I got into it three years ago.
Present Perfect Experience or duration up to now I have been swimming since childhood.

Polite Ways to End a Conversation

  • "I should go and get another drink."
  • "I need to find my friend."
  • "I should make a phone call."
  • "It was great talking to you."
  • "Let's continue this later."

Reflection and Error Correction

Self-check questions:

  • Can I use present simple to talk about my hobbies?
  • Can I use past simple to say when I started a hobby?
  • Can I use present perfect to talk about how long I have done something?
  • Can I start and end a conversation politely?

Common Errors to Avoid

  • Incorrect: I am swim in my free time. → Correct: I swim in my free time.
  • Incorrect: I get into golf three years ago. → Correct: I got into golf three years ago.
  • Incorrect: I have been swim since childhood. → Correct: I have been swimming since childhood.

Key Expressions for Conversations

  • Starting: "Hi there. Is this seat taken?" / "Nice to meet you."
  • Asking about hobbies: "What do you do in your free time?"
  • Ending: "It was great talking to you." / "See you around."

Teacher Notes: Making Small Talk

Aims

  • Main aim: Students will be able to start, maintain, and end a conversation using present simple, past simple, and present perfect to discuss hobbies and interests.
  • Sub-aims: Students will use social expressions for breaking the ice and polite closings. Students will ask and answer questions about hobbies and interests.
  • Level: B1
  • Timing: 80-90 minutes

Procedure

  1. Warm-Up (7-9 min): Students discuss the three questions with a partner. Elicit a few answers. Introduce the topic and objective.
  2. Context (8-10 min): Read the dialogue. Students identify the opening, small talk topics, and closing. Discuss the conversation structure.
  3. Comprehension Questions (5-7 min): Students answer individually. Check answers in pairs. Elicit answers from the class.
  4. Vocabulary Focus (5-7 min): Introduce the vocabulary bank. Students categorise hobbies, sports, and interests. Practice pronunciation.
  5. Grammar Focus: Present Simple (5-7 min): Present examples from the dialogue. Elicit form and use. Students create their own examples.
  6. Grammar Focus: Past Simple & Present Perfect (5-7 min): Contrast the two tenses. Use the timeline visual. Focus on time expressions (ago, since, for).
  7. Controlled Practice 1 (5-7 min): Students complete Conversation 1 with a partner. Monitor and support.
  8. Controlled Practice 2 (5-7 min): Students complete Conversation 2 with a partner. Focus on natural intonation.
  9. Controlled Practice 3 (3-5 min): Students complete Conversation 3 with a partner. Practice polite closings.
  10. Free Practice: Create Your Own Conversation (10-12 min): Students prepare a new dialogue. Perform for the class or in groups. Provide feedback.
  11. Reflection and Error Correction (5-7 min): Review common errors. Self-check questions. Summary of key expressions.

  4. B has been swimming since childhood.

  6. A visited Brazil and Colombia.

  8. Answers will vary.

Checking Questions

  • What is small talk?
  • How do you start a conversation with someone new?
  • What tense do we use for hobbies? (Present simple)
  • When do we use past simple? (Completed actions)
  • When do we use present perfect? (Experiences and duration)
  • How do you end a conversation politely?

Likely Errors

  • Using present simple for past actions: I get into golf three years ago. → I got into golf three years ago.
  • Using past simple for duration: I swam since childhood. → I have been swimming since childhood.
  • Forgetting auxiliary verbs in present perfect: I been swimming. → I have been swimming.

Extension Ideas

  • Writing extension: Students write a short email to a new friend describing their hobbies and interests.
  • Role play: Students practice small talk at different events (dinner, party, training course).
  • Vocabulary building: Expand the vocabulary bank with more hobbies and interests.
